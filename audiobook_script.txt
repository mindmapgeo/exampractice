PART 1: PROTOCOLS — ALL 10 INTERPRETER PROTOCOLS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 1: GREETING PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Greeting Protocol is mandatory for every call you take.

When you connect with the PROVIDER, you must include these four mandatory elements:
Number one: Your interpreter's name.
Number two: The language you interpret — Ukrainian.
Number three: Your interpreter ID number.
Number four: The statement that everything in the session will be interpreted.

The script to the provider is:
"Hello, this is [your name], your Ukrainian interpreter, ID [your number]. Everything in the session will be interpreted. How can I help you?"

When you connect with the PATIENT, you must include these four mandatory elements:
Number one: Your interpreter's name.
Number two: The language being interpreted into.
Number three: That everything will be kept confidential.
Number four: That everything in the session will be interpreted.

The script to the patient in Ukrainian is:
"Добрий день, мене звати [ваше ім'я], я ваш перекладач англійської мови. Усе під час цієї сесії буде перекладатися та залишатиметься конфіденційним."

Optional add-ons you may include:
• "Address questions to each other, not to you."
• "I may ask end users to reposition the device for optimal setup."
• "Speak in short sentences."
• "Speak in a loud and clear tone, pausing often."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 2: CLOSING PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The closing is different for audio calls versus video calls.

FOR AUDIO CALLS — 4 steps:
Step 1: Offer additional assistance — this is MANDATORY. Say: "Is there anything else I can help you with? Do you need help with something else?"
Step 2: State your Interpreter's ID number. Say: "This is interpreter ID number [your number]."
Step 3: Say goodbye to the doctor. Say: "Thank you for calling. Have a great day!"
Step 4: Say goodbye to the patient in Ukrainian: "Гарного вам дня, пане або пані."

FOR VIDEO CALLS — 4 steps:
Step 1: Offer additional assistance — this is MANDATORY.
Step 2: Remind the doctor to rate the call. Say: "Please remember to rate the call after we hang up. Your feedback is valuable and appreciated."
Step 3: Say goodbye to the doctor.
Step 4: Say goodbye to the patient in Ukrainian: "Гарного вам дня, пане або пані."

KEY DIFFERENCE: In audio calls, step 2 is stating your ID number. In video calls, step 2 is reminding to rate the call.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 3: INTERVENTION — THE 3-STEP PROCESS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When you need to intervene to ask for repetition, clarification, or verification, always follow this 3-step process.

Step 1: Identify yourself as the interpreter. Speak first to the party that will be waiting for you to get clarification.

Step 2: Identify yourself as the interpreter to the other party. Then request clarification, repetition, or whatever you need. Say to the required party: "Interpreter speaking, can you please repeat or clarify?"

Step 3: Resume interpreting normally.

When speaking to the opposite party, say: "This is the interpreter speaking. Excuse me, the interpreter needs to clarify."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 4: 3RD PERSON PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Normally you interpret in FIRST PERSON — meaning you say what the provider says directly, as if you are the provider speaking.

If the provider or patient speaks to you directly instead of to each other, you must intervene and remind them to speak to each other.

To the PATIENT in Ukrainian:
"Пане або пані, я перекладач. Будь ласка, звертайтеся безпосередньо до лікаря, щоб уникнути двозначностей і забезпечити точність повідомлення."

To the PROVIDER in English:
"This is the interpreter. May I please ask you to speak directly to your patient so that we can ensure accuracy and avoid confusion?"

IMPORTANT: If they CONTINUE to speak to you after your reminder, you must continue to interpret in FIRST PERSON regardless.

ALWAYS specify the reason for using 3rd person in the Call Notes.

EXCEPTIONS — 3rd person IS ALLOWED for these cases:
Exception 1: Very young children — aged 7 or younger.
Exception 2: Emergencies — when you need to summarize.
Exception 3: Anyone who is confused — such as dementia patients, substance abuse cases, or post-surgery patients.
Exception 4: Mentally ill patients.
Exception 5: Trauma triggered by situations that arise during the call.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 5: THE SAY NO MODEL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When someone makes an inappropriate request — such as offering you a gift, asking for your personal schedule, or asking for a sight translation — you use the Say No Model.

The 3 steps are:
Step 1: Be gracious.
Step 2: Offer 2 or 3 alternatives.
Step 3: Give a reason.

Example 1 — Gift scenario:
Caller says: "Interpreter, you have been amazing. Can I send you a gift?"
You say: "Thank you for your appreciation of my service. Maybe you can donate this to the hospital and they can make better use of it. Or maybe you can call customer service and let them know about my performance. Your appreciation for my job and kind words are more than enough for me."

Example 2 — Working hours scenario:
Caller says: "What are your working hours? I want to request you again."
You say: "Thank you for your appreciation of my service. All of our interpreters are qualified, and you can call the line at any time since we have service 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. My hours can change so it wouldn't be something certain. I do appreciate your words, and you can always leave feedback about my performance."

Example 3 — Sight translation scenario:
Caller says: "Could you please do a sight translation of this document?"
You say: "Thank you for trusting my language skills, and I understand that you need the information in this document to be transmitted. As an interpreter, I can help you read it out loud and I'll interpret as you do. Or maybe you can give me a summary of the document orally and I can interpret it as well. I'm stating this due to company policy."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 6: HOLD PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STANDARD 10-MINUTE HOLD:
You can hold up to 10 minutes for a provider to return. Pause your video when there is no provider in the room.

The 5 steps for a standard hold are:
Step 1: Let the provider know you can only wait up to 10 minutes.
Step 2: Inform the patient of the hold.
Step 3: Turn the camera off and mute your audio.
Step 4: Make a notation in the Call Notes box.
Step 5: Report on Discord.

Script to the PROVIDER: "This is the interpreter. I need to let you know that I have a 10-minute hold policy. I'll be here with my camera off until you come back."

Script to the PATIENT in Ukrainian: "Пане або пані, з вами говорить перекладач. Я вимкну свою камеру з міркувань безпеки до повернення лікаря. Я слухатиму все, що буде сказано."

FLEXIBLE HOLD — when provider asks for more than 10 minutes:
• If they give a SPECIFIC amount of time: honor that amount of time.
• If they give an UNSPECIFIED or uncountable amount of time: hold for 20 minutes.
• If it is a LIFE OR DEATH situation: hold indefinitely, for as long as needed.

All flexible hold cases must be noted in Call Notes and reported on Discord.

BLUE OR BLACK SCREEN — 1-minute rule:
At the beginning of a call, if you get a blue or black screen, you have 60 seconds to get a response.

Check whether anyone is connected. Say: "Hello, this is your Ukrainian interpreter. Is there anybody on the line?"

If there is no response after 60 seconds, say: "This is your Ukrainian interpreter. I'm unable to hear anyone on the line. I will disconnect from the call. I apologize for the inconvenience. Goodbye."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 7: TROUBLESHOOTING PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

If there are technical issues such as poor audio or video quality, follow these 3 steps in order.

Step 1: Ask the provider to move the device closer or change the positioning. Or, turn your camera off and immediately back on to see if the problem is resolved.

Say: "This is the interpreter, [provider name], there are some technical issues. Allow me to troubleshoot."

Step 2: If the problem is not resolved, keep the camera off and continue with the interpretation.

Step 3: If these steps are not successful, ask the provider if you may connect them to another interpreter. Provide the Technical Support number: 1-866-449-4428.

Say: "This is the interpreter, [provider name], we are still experiencing some technical issues. May I connect you with another interpreter to see if this helps? I apologize for the inconvenience."

The Technical Support number is: 1-866-449-4428. One. Eight. Six. Six. Four. Four. Nine. Four. Four. Two. Eight.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 8: DIAL-OUT PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

If you are asked to place a call, you must collect this information BEFORE you dial.

Ask the provider these 5 questions:

Question 1: "What is the phone number you need me to dial?"
Question 2: "Who should I say is calling?"
Question 3: "May I please have the name of the person or patient we're calling?"
Question 4: "Should I leave a message in case we reach a voicemail box?"
Question 5: "Is there a callback number, in case the patient calls back?"

Special note on Direct Access Line calls, also called DAL calls: These appear as standard audio calls. When the call sheet loads, check the Account field — it will say Direct Access Line or DAL. In some DAL calls, the patient may offer you a phone number. Take that number and proceed with the dial-out protocol.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 9: LONG CALLS AND WITHDRAWAL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

AT 90 MINUTES:
For any call lasting 90 minutes or longer, it is recommended that you self-monitor your performance and make the decision to transfer the call if needed.

Signs that you need to transfer include:
Sign A: Increased fatigue.
Sign B: Performance concerns.
Sign C: Emotional distress responses.
Sign D: Need to use the restroom or drink water.

Script to the PROVIDER: "[Provider name], as the interpreter, I think we've reached a point in the conversation to switch out interpreters. Please remain on the line and you'll be connected to one of my colleagues momentarily. Thank you."

Script to the PATIENT in Ukrainian: "Як перекладач, вважаю, що ми дійшли до моменту розмови, коли доцільно змінити перекладача. Будь ласка, залишайтеся на лінії — вас негайно з'єднають з одним із моїх колег. Дякую."

AT 120 MINUTES:
You are expected to transfer the call so you can take a break.

IMPORTANT RULES:
Rule 1: The end of your shift alone is NOT a valid reason to withdraw. Do not mention your shift to the callers.
Rule 2: If you don't feel tired at 120 minutes, you may choose to remain on the call.
Rule 3: Always make a notation in the Call Notes box.
Rule 4: Report on Discord and keep your Team Leader informed.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 10: SHADOWING BETWEEN LANGUAGES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When the provider speaks Ukrainian, or when the patient starts speaking English, you may stay on the line in a shadowing role.

Script to the PROVIDER: "This is the interpreter. I can tell you speak Ukrainian — or — your patient speaks some English. I will stay here shadowing in case you need me. I'll be paying attention and will jump in if there's any piece of information you deem very important."

Script to the PATIENT in Ukrainian: "З вами говорить перекладач. Оскільки лікар розмовляє українською мовою, або ви можете спілкуватися англійською, я залишатимусь на лінії та спостерігатиму на випадок, якщо буду потрібен. Я уважно стежитиму за розмовою та втручатимусь, якщо буде повідомлено важливу інформацію."

While shadowing, you must:
• Remain alert and keep taking notes as if you were interpreting.
• Be ready if you are summoned at any point.
• Pay attention in case there is a misunderstanding, and be proactive if an intervention is needed.
• Make sure all parties are aware of your presence.
• Be transparent at all times.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 11: DUAL INTERPRETATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Dual interpretation is requested when a patient — typically a minor — uses ASL to communicate while the parent speaks a different language.

How it works:
Step 1: Whoever connects to the call first adds the other needed language interpreter.
Step 2: Put the provider on a brief hold while you brief your teammate on the session.
Step 3: The ASL interpreter takes the lead on the interpretation and manages call flow and pausing.

Key rules:
• If the provider primarily interacts with the deaf patient, allow the interaction to occur and take notes before asking to pause.
• When clarifying, identify yourself as "this is the Ukrainian interpreter."
• Always interpret each other's interpretation.
• Be transparent about all interactions with each party.
• Make a note in Call Notes that this was a dual team interpretation.
• Report it on Discord as well.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHAPTER 12: CALL NOTES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Always make sure to leave a note during your call, even if nothing eventful happened.

Rules for Call Notes:
Rule 1: Information must be accurate and grammatically correct.
Rule 2: Don't be afraid to ask for spelling.
Rule 3: Names must be written with CAPITAL LETTERS for initials.

When someone refuses to provide full information:
• If they provide initials, type the initials.
• If they still refuse, type N/A in CAPITAL letters.
• Leave a note in the Call Notes box about them refusing to provide information.
