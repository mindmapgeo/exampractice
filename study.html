Greeting Protocol
Mandatory Scripted
▼

The Equiti Standards VMI Assessment requires these exact elements at the start of every call.

MANDATORY elements for the PROVIDER:
  • ✓
    Interpreter's name
  • ✓
    Language you interpret in
  • ✓
    Interpreter's ID #
  • ✓
    Everything will be interpreted
MANDATORY elements for the LEP (patient):
  • ✓
    Interpreter's name
  • ✓
    Language being interpreted into
  • ✓
    Everything will be kept confidential
  • ✓
    Everything in the session will be interpreted
🇺🇸 To Provider (English)
"Hello, this is _______, your Ukrainian interpreter, ID XXXXX. Everything in the session will be interpreted. How can I help you? / Is there anything I need to know about the session?"
🇺🇦 To Patient (Ukrainian)
"Добрий день, мене звати ___, я ваш перекладач англійської мови. Усе під час цієї сесії буде перекладатися та залишатиметься конфіденційним."
Optional Add-ons:
  • +
    "Address questions to each other, not to you."
  • +
    "I may ask end users to re-position the device for optimal setup."
  • +
    "Speak in short sentences."
  • +
    "Speak in a loud and clear tone, pausing often."

Closing Protocol
Mandatory Scripted
▼

Different steps for AUDIO vs VIDEO calls. Both end with farewell to LEP in Ukrainian.

📞 AUDIO Call (4 steps):
  • Offer additional assistance (MANDATORY)
  • State Interpreter's ID #
  • Say goodbye to doctor
  • Say goodbye to the LEP
📹 VIDEO Call (4 steps):
  • Offer additional assistance (MANDATORY)
  • Remind doctor to rate the call
  • Say goodbye to doctor
  • Say goodbye to the LEP
🇺🇸 Audio Closing Script
(1) "Is there anything else I can help you with? / Do you need help with something else?"
(2) "This is interpreter ID# __________."
(3) "Thank you for calling / Thank you for using our services. Have a great day!"
(4) "Гарного вам дня, пане / пані."
📹 Video Closing Script
(1) "Is there anything else I can help you with? / Do you need help with something else?"
(2) "Please remember to rate the call after we hang up. Your feedback is valuable and appreciated."
(3) "Thank you for calling. Have a great day!"
(4) "Гарного вам дня, пане / пані."

Intervention Protocol (3-Step)
Scripted
▼

When you need to ask for repetition, clarification, or verification. Use this 3-step process.

  • Identify yourself as the interpreter. Speak first to the party that will be waiting.
  • Identify yourself as the interpreter to the other party. Request clarification/repetition/etc.
  • Resume interpreting.
🇺🇸 Intervention Script (to opposite party)
"This is the interpreter speaking / Excuse me, the interpreter needs to clarify / a repetition / etc."
🇺🇸 Then to required party
"Interpreter speaking, can you please repeat/clarify....?"

3rd Person Protocol
Critical
▼

When parties speak to you directly instead of each other, intervene and remind them. Note reason in Call Notes.

⚠️ EXCEPTIONS — When 3rd person IS allowed:
  • !
    Very young children (age 7 or younger)
  • !
    Emergencies (summarizing)
  • !
    Anyone who is confused (dementia / substance abuse / post surgery)
  • !
    Mentally ill patients
  • !
    Trauma triggered by situations in the call
🇺🇦 To Patient (Ukrainian)
"Пане / пані, я перекладач. Будь ласка, звертайтеся безпосередньо до лікаря, щоб уникнути двозначностей і забезпечити точність повідомлення."
🇺🇸 To Provider (English)
"This is the interpreter, [provider], may I please ask you to speak directly to your patient so that we can ensure accuracy and avoid confusion?"
📝 RULE
If they CONTINUE speaking to you — continue to interpret in FIRST person regardless. Specify reason in Call Notes.

Hold Protocol
Timing Critical Critical
▼
✅ Standard = 10 min
⚡ Provider gives specific time = honor it
❓ Unspecified time = 20 min
🚨 Life or death = hold indefinitely
🔵 Blue/black screen = 60 seconds
Standard 10-Minute Hold — 5 Steps:
  • Let the provider know we can only wait up to 10 minutes.
  • Inform the LEP of the hold.
  • Turn the camera off and mute audio.
  • Make a notation on Call Notes.
  • Report on Discord.
🇺🇸 To Provider
"This is the interpreter; I need to let you know that I have a 10-minute hold policy. I'll be here with my camera off until you come back."
🇺🇦 To Patient
"Пане / пані, з вами говорить перекладач. Я вимкну свою камеру з міркувань безпеки до повернення лікаря. Я слухатиму все, що буде сказано (за потреби)."
🔵 Blue/Black Screen (1-Minute Hold):
🇺🇸 Step 1 — Check for response (60 sec window)
"Hello, this is your Ukrainian interpreter. Is there anybody on the line?"
🇺🇸 Step 2 — If no response
"This is your Ukrainian interpreter. I'm unable to hear anyone on the line. I will disconnect from the call. I apologize for the inconvenience. Goodbye."

Troubleshooting Protocol
Scripted
▼

For poor audio/video quality. Follow these 3 steps in order.

  • Ask provider to move device closer / change positioning OR turn camera off then back on.
  • If unresolved: keep camera off and continue interpretation.
  • If still unresolved: ask if you may connect to another interpreter. Provide Tech Support: 1-866-449-4428
🇺🇸 Step 1 Script
"This is the interpreter, [provider], there are some technical issues. Allow me to troubleshoot."
🇺🇸 Step 3 Script
"This is the interpreter, [provider], we are still experiencing some technical issues. May I connect you with another interpreter to see if this helps? I apologize for the inconvenience."
📞 Tech Support: 1-866-449-4428

Dial-Out Protocol
Scripted
▼

Before dialing out, always collect this information from the provider:

  • "What is the phone number you need me to dial?"
  • "Who should I say is calling?"
  • "May I please have the name of the person / patient we're calling?"
  • "Should I leave a message in case we reach a voicemail box?"
  • "Is there a callback number? (In case not stated before)"
📝 DAL NOTE
Direct Access Line (DAL) calls appear as standard audio calls. Check the Account field for "Direct Access Line" or "DAL". If the LEP offers a phone number, take it and proceed with dial-out.

Long Calls & Withdrawal Protocol
Timing Critical Critical
▼
⚠️ 90 min → Self-monitor, consider transfer
🚨 120 min → Expected to transfer
Signs you need to transfer (90 min):
  • !
    Increased fatigue
  • !
    Performance concerns
  • !
    Emotional distress responses
  • !
    Need to use restroom or drink water
🇺🇸 To Provider (90+ min transfer)
"[Provider], as the interpreter, I think we've reached a point in the conversation to switch out interpreters. Please remain on the line and you'll be connected to one of my colleagues momentarily. Thank you."
🇺🇦 To Patient
"Як перекладач, вважаю, що ми дійшли до моменту розмови, коли доцільно змінити перекладача. Будь ласка, залишайтеся на лінії — вас негайно з'єднають з одним із моїх колег. Дякую."
⚠️ Key Rules:
  • !
    End of shift alone is NOT a valid reason to withdraw
  • !
    If not tired at 120 min, you CAN remain
  • !
    Always note in Call Notes box
  • !
    Report on Discord; keep TL informed

Dual Interpretation Protocol
Scripted
▼

When the patient (typically a minor) uses ASL while the parent speaks another language.

  • Whoever connects first adds the other needed language interpreter.
  • Put provider on brief hold to brief your team on the session.
  • The ASL interpreter takes the lead on interpretation and call flow.
Key Rules:
  • ✓
    If provider interacts with deaf patient — allow interaction, take notes before asking to pause.
  • ✓
    Identify yourself as "this is the (Ukrainian) interpreter" when clarifying.
  • ✓
    Always interpret each other's interpretation.
  • ✓
    Be transparent about all interactions.
  • !
    Make a note in Call Notes that this was a dual team interpretation.
  • !
    Report it on Discord.

"Say No" Model & Inappropriate Requests
Scripted Critical
▼

When someone makes an inappropriate request (gifts, personal hours, sight translation, etc.):

3 Steps:
  • Be gracious
  • Offer 2 or 3 alternatives
  • Give a reason
🇺🇸 Gift Scenario
Caller: "Interpreter you have been amazing... can I send you a gift?"

You: "Thank you for your appreciation of my service. Maybe you can donate this to the hospital, and they can make better use of it / maybe you can call customer service and let them know about my performance. Your appreciation for my job and kind words are more than enough for me."
🇺🇸 Working Hours Scenario
Caller: "What are your working hours?"

You: "Thank you for your appreciation of my service. All of our interpreters are qualified, and you can call the line at any time since we have service 24/7. My hours can be changed so it wouldn't be something certain. I do appreciate your words, and you can always leave feedback about my performance."
🇺🇸 Sight Translation Scenario
Caller: "Could you please do a sight translation of this document?"

You: "Thank you for trusting my language skills and I understand that you need the information on this document to be transmitted. As an interpreter I can help you read it out loud and I'll interpret as you do, or maybe you can give me a summary of the document orally and I can interpret it as well. I'm stating this due to company policy."
👁️
Shadowing Between Languages
Scripted
▼

When the provider speaks Ukrainian or the patient starts speaking English, you can stay on the line shadowing.

🇺🇸 To Provider
"This is the interpreter, I can tell you speak Ukrainian / your patient speaks some English. I will stay here shadowing in case you need me. I'll be paying attention and will jump in if there's any piece of information you deem very important."
🇺🇦 To Patient
"З вами говорить перекладач. Оскільки лікар розмовляє українською мовою / ви можете спілкуватися англійською, я залишатимусь на лінії та спостерігатиму на випадок, якщо буду потрібен(потрібна). Я уважно стежитиму за розмовою та втручатимусь, якщо буде повідомлено важливу інформацію."
Key Rules:
  • ✓
    Remain alert, keep taking notes as if interpreting.
  • ✓
    Be ready if summoned.
  • ✓
    Be proactive if a misunderstanding occurs.
  • ✓
    Make sure all parties know your presence.
  • ✓
    Be transparent at all times.

Call Notes Protocol
Mandatory
▼

Always leave a note during your call, even if nothing eventful happened.

  • ✓
    Information must be accurate and grammatically correct.
  • ✓
    Don't be afraid to ask for spelling.
  • ✓
    Names use CAPITAL LETTERS / UPPER CASE for initials.
When data is refused:
  • !
    If they provide initials → type the initials.
  • !
    If they still refuse → type N/A in CAPITALS.
  • !
    Leave a note that they refused to provide information.
📋 Always Note These Events on Discord
• Any hold over 10 minutes
• Long calls approaching/exceeding 90 or 120 minutes
• Dual interpretation sessions
• Flexible hold situations
• Life/death situations
